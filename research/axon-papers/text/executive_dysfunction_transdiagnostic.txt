                                          1 Introduction
                                              Mental health conditions have traditionally been classified using categorical diagnostic sys-
                                          tems, most notably the Diagnostic and Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders (DSM) of the
                                          American Psychiatric Association and the International Classification of Diseases (ICD) of the
                                          World Health Organization. These frameworks define disorders based on clusters of observable
                                          symptoms, offering important advantages for clinical communication, service organization, and
                                          diagnostic standardization. However, such classification systems do not fully align with the under-
                                          lying cognitive and neurobiological mechanisms that often cut across diagnostic boundaries.
