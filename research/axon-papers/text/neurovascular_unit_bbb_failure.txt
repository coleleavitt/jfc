Abstract:
Alzheimer’s disease (AD) is a progressive neurodegenerative disorder traditionally defined by amyloid-β deposition and tau
pathology. Recent research highlights the role of blood–brain barrier (BBB) breakdown and neurovascular unit (NVU) dysfunction in
disease initiation and progression. These factors contribute to neuroinflammation, cerebral hypoperfusion and impaired waste
clearance. Despite this, the relationship between BBB integrity, NVU function and cognitive decline in AD remains inadequately
understood. Data shows that blood–brain barrier disruption and neurovascular unit dysfunction are critical predictors of cognitive
decline in Alzheimer’s disease.

Keywords: Blood–brain barrier (BBB), neurovascular unit (NVU), Alzheimer’s disease (AD), cognitive impairment, cerebral perfusion
